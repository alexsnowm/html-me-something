In the beginning...

Give a kid some LEGO bricks and they'll build for a day - or a couple days - but give a kid a camera and the Internet too, and they'll post what they built online for the rest of time.

Living In A Cave

I started out with a camera I found in my parents' house. It was the type that saved its pictures to a floppy disk. As can be seen, the picture quality was a bit grainy, and it was terrible at taking close-ups or macro photos.

Discovering Fire

So I begged my parents to get a proper DSLR camera.

A Rising Civilization

I participated in online forums with other LEGO enthusiasts and started paying more attention to stuff that other people built, so my own building started getting better.

The Renaissance

Things got larger and more advanced. And I started trying my hand at making stories with LEGO.

The Industrial Revolution

Getting more detailed...

The Space Race

As my LEGO collection wasn't getting much larger, I tried using more and more unconventional techniques to stretch my collection and do some of my most complicated building yet. I also started experimenting with Photoshop to make neater-looking comics.

Modern Times

I enter the era of the moving image and start attempting stop-motion animation with LEGO. What will the future hold?
